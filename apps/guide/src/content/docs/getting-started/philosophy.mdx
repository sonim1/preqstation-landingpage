title: Product Philosophy
description: Why PREQSTATION exists and what kind of workflow it is built for.
---

PREQSTATION exists for developers who cannot sit at the computer all day but still want software work to keep moving. AI can already produce rough implementation quickly. The harder problem is preparing the work, dispatching it safely, and coming back to something you can actually review and ship.

## Dispatch Anywhere

Useful work does not only appear while you are at your desk. It shows up while you are commuting, in meetings, or between other responsibilities.

PREQSTATION is built so you can capture the task when the idea is fresh, shape it with acceptance criteria, and dispatch it when you have the right moment. The point is not constant activity. The point is not losing momentum every time you leave the keyboard.

## Built for Solo & Small Teams

Most project tools assume a larger human team and a heavier process layer. That can be useful, but it is not the problem PREQSTATION is trying to solve.

PREQSTATION is for the part of the workflow where a task is already clear enough to execute. Keep backlog planning and broad coordination in GitHub Issues, Linear, Jira, Notion, or wherever they already work for you. PREQSTATION stays focused on scoped execution work, review signals, and visible follow-through.

## From MVP to Production

The first draft is cheaper than it used to be. AI can get you to an MVP quickly. What still costs time is turning that draft into a change you trust.

PREQSTATION adds the guardrails around that transition: isolated worktrees, explicit ownership, reviewable notes, verification output, and a task record that tells you what happened instead of forcing you to reconstruct it from terminal scrollback.

## How This Maps to the Technical Workflow

The fast mental model is simple: queued, working, done.

The real workflow remains more precise:

- `inbox` for newly captured work
- `todo` for work ready to execute
- `hold` for blocked execution
- `ready` for work waiting on review
- `done` for verified completion

`queued` and `working` are still run-state overlays, not replacements for those workflow states.

If you want the lower-level model and the kitchen metaphor, continue with [Kitchen Metaphor](/guide/getting-started/concepts).
